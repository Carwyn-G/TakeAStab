someUser24

Bobbin thread keeps making messy knots on the bottom of my fabric?

I just got my first sewing machine and started doing some test stitches, but the thread keeps bunching up underneath my fabric? any advice?

craftyguy99

sounds like a thread tension issue. usually called "birdnesting" when this happens. adjust the tension on your machine and you should be good to go!

imsewcool

That's definitely a problem with thread tension. Look for a numbered dial on your machine and set it a little higher to adjust the top thread tension. If that doesn't fix it you'll have to adjust the bobbin tension, usually there's a small screw in the bobbin casing that you can tighten slightly to resolve it. Hope this helps, congrats on your first sewing machine!

French knots are the bane of my existence!!!

I'm begging y'all, if you have any tips for french knots let me hear 'em because I just cannot get the hang of them.

embroideryKing

They can be tricky when your starting out. Make sure you arent wrapping the thread too many times around the needle. And you want to leave a space when you put the needle back through the fabric. don't go back through the same hole you came up from.

stitch

mind ur tension. its hard to explain without just practicin to get the hang of it, but you gotta keep the thread pretty snug while you're working. use ur free hand to hold the thread as you pull the knot so you dont get a bunch of slack that can cause problems

needlebloke

Practice practice practice. Go slow with them, and you'll get there.
